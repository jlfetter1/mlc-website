## Patterns of the Top 10%

### 1. Social Proof Is Non-Negotiable
Every top-performing site displays **client logos prominently** above the fold or in a dedicated trust bar. be School leads with Danone, KPMG, World Bank, Mercedes-Benz. IH Mexico shows Abbott, Siemens Energy, Avianca. MLC has **zero visible client logos or testimonials** — this is the single biggest gap.

### 2. Modern Design = Modern Company
be School (Tailwind + Figtree) is the clear design winner. IH Mexico (Poppins + indigo) feels contemporary. The bottom performers (Corporate English, Fluency Corp) look dated. **Design quality signals service quality** in this niche where clients are corporate HR/L&D teams making vendor decisions.

### 3. Clear Process Visualization
IH Mexico's 4-step process (Diagnóstico > Agrupación > Objetivos > Seguimiento) and BBE's tiered programs (Empower/Live/Access) give prospects a clear mental model of what working with the company looks like. **Process = professionalism.**

### 4. Free Entry Point Lowers the Barrier
- IH Mexico: "Diagnóstico sin costo" (free diagnostic)
- be School: "Test Your Level" (free assessment)
- British Council: "Book a consultation"
- Fluency Corp: "Free consultation"
All top sites offer a **zero-risk first step**. MLC does this with their "Cotizar ya!" but should make the free aspect more explicit.

### 5. HR-Focused Messaging Wins B2B
IH Mexico dedicates an entire section to HR metrics: "Capacitación en inglés con métricas claras para Recursos Humanos" with reporting, KPIs, and ROI measurement. be School mentions HR monitoring. **B2B buyers are HR and L&D teams** — they need to justify spend. Reporting and measurable outcomes close deals.
