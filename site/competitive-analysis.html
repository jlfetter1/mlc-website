Executive Summary

Modern Language Center operates in a competitive B2B corporate language training market in Mexico, with 20+ years of experience and a strong service portfolio. However, the current website significantly underperforms against top competitors in visual design, social proof, and SEO — the three factors most critical to winning B2B vendor evaluations.

The top competitor, be School, sets the standard with a modern Tailwind-built site, tier-1 client logos (Danone, KPMG, World Bank), and testimonials with company attribution. International House Mexico leads in process clarity and HR-focused messaging.

MLC's biggest opportunities: add visible social proof, modernize the design language, and build HR-focused messaging around reporting and ROI — the decision-making factors that B2B buyers care about most.

SEO Gaps & Recommendations

  • Missing blog/content hub: IH Mexico and Fluency Corp rank well because they publish content. MLC has no blog.
  • Weak meta titles: MLC's homepage title is a long description, not a keyword-targeted title.
  • No schema markup: Missing EducationalOrganization and Course schema that competitors use.
  • Bilingual SEO conflict: Spanish and English pages compete without proper hreflang tags.
  • No FAQ content: IH Mexico's 12+ FAQs generate long-tail traffic. MLC has none.
04

Patterns of the Top 10%

1

Social Proof Is Non-Negotiable

Every top-performing site displays client logos prominently above the fold. be School leads with Danone, KPMG, World Bank, Mercedes-Benz. IH Mexico shows Abbott, Siemens Energy, Avianca. MLC has zero visible client logos or testimonials — the single biggest gap to close.

2

Modern Design = Modern Company

be School's Tailwind build with Figtree font and bold yellow/pink palette signals innovation. The weakest competitors (Corporate English) look dated. Design quality is a proxy for service quality when HR teams are comparing vendors in browser tabs.

3

Clear Process Visualization

IH Mexico's 4-step process and BBE's tiered programs give prospects a clear mental model. When a buyer sees numbered steps with outcomes, they see professionalism and can envision the engagement before it starts.

4

Free Entry Point Lowers the Barrier

Every top site offers a zero-risk first step: free diagnostic, free level test, free consultation. MLC's "Cotizar ya!" pushes toward a quote — it should lead with a free, no-commitment action instead.

5

HR-Focused Messaging Wins B2B

IH Mexico dedicates an entire section to HR metrics: reporting, KPIs, and ROI measurement. B2B buyers are HR and L&D teams who must justify spend. MLC needs to speak directly to this decision-maker with data and accountability language.

05

Recommended Design Direction

Color Palette

Keep MLC's brand blue #008AFE as primary. Refine with a deeper navy #1a2744 for text and headers, warm the background to #f8f9fb, and add a gold accent #B08C5A for premium CTAs — already present in the current site's secondary button.

Typography

Replace MarselisPro with a modern pairing: Instrument Serif for editorial headlines (signals sophistication) + DM Sans for body copy (clean, highly legible). Both are free Google Fonts with excellent language support.

Layout & Structure

Move from Elementor page-builder layout to a streamlined, component-driven design. Large hero with motion, trust bar of client logos, clear section rhythm with dark/light alternation. Process steps visualized, not buried in paragraphs.

Animation & Motion

GSAP + ScrollTrigger for scroll-driven section reveals, parallax depth on hero, and micro-interactions on buttons and cards. A 3D scroll-stop hero asset will immediately differentiate MLC from every competitor — none of them use motion design.

What Every Competitor Gets Wrong

  • No animation or motion design: Zero competitors use scroll-triggered animation. This is MLC's opportunity to instantly look world-class.
  • Template-heavy builds: Most use WordPress + Elementor. A custom HTML/GSAP build will load 3-5x faster and feel premium.
  • Weak differentiation: Everyone says "personalized" and "experienced." None tell a compelling brand story with visual conviction.
  • No video: Zero competitors use video content on their landing pages. A scroll-driven 3D asset would be a category-defining differentiator.
